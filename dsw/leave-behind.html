Natural language queries account for a growing share of search; systems that fail NLP intent return irrelevant results and lose the sale
Algolia solution: Algolia NeuralSearch understands natural language intent — gender, price constraints, occasion, and lifestyle context — returning results matching what the shopper means, not just what they typed. Case study →

No Federated Search — Content & Loyalty Invisible
A DSW customer typing 'rewards' in the search bar gets unrelated products — not the VIP loyalty program page. Help content, store locator, brand editorial, and loyalty information are completely invisible in search.
Algolia solution: Algolia Federated Search indexes multiple data sources — product catalog, help center, loyalty pages, brand editorial, store locator — and surfaces the right content type in SAYT and full results. Case study →

Typo Tolerance Requires User Click — Not Auto-Correcting
A customer typing 'sneackers' must read a banner and click 'Sneakers' to see results. On mobile, they may not notice the banner and assume DSW has no sneakers.
Algolia solution: Algolia auto-corrects typos transparently — showing 'Results for sneakers' inline with zero extra clicks. Mobile users see corrected results immediately. Case study →

Recommendations Are Brand-Siloed, Not Collaborative
When a customer views a Birkenstock sandal, DSW shows only other Birkenstock sandals — missing the chance to sell matching accessories, insoles, or complementary brands the customer also buys.
Algolia solution: Algolia Recommend uses collaborative filtering across the full catalog — surfacing what customers who bought THIS product also bought, regardless of brand. Case study →
