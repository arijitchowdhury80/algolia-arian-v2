CRITICAL
False Positives on Gibberish — Search Returns Wrong Products
Costco's search has no minimum relevance threshold. Nonsense queries surface unrelated products, destroying member trust. When a member misspells a complex query, they may see garbage results instead of a helpful empty state.
Algolia solution: Algolia's relevance engine uses minimum match thresholds and confidence scoring — gibberish returns a true empty state with contextual suggestions. No false positives. Case study →

CRITICAL
No Federated Search — Help Content on Separate Domain
Members looking for Costco's famous return policy are ejected from the shopping experience to a separate domain. Costco Travel, Business Center, and support content are invisible to the main search bar — creating channel silos across a $27B+ digitally-enabled sales operation.
Algolia solution: Algolia Federated Search indexes multiple content types — products, policies, travel, help — in a single search bar with clear category sections. Members never leave the shopping context. Case study →

CRITICAL
NLP / Conversational Queries Blocked by WAF
Complex natural language queries — exactly the type 48% of mobile app users type — are blocked before reaching the search engine. Members who type 'healthy snacks for kids' or 'wireless earbuds with noise cancellation under 200' get Access Denied. WAF configuration and lack of NLP infrastructure are both failure modes.
Algolia solution: Algolia NeuralSearch parses multi-attribute intent natively without complex URL parameters that trigger WAF rules. Sub-100ms response, no WAF exposure, no special infrastructure required. Case study →

CRITICAL
Typo Tolerance Works But Silent — No 'Did You Mean'
Costco's typo engine works but communicates nothing. A member who types 'kirkand' (common Kirkland misspelling) gets results but has no idea why — and may assume the results are for a different query entirely. Trust in search is eroded silently.
Algolia solution: Algolia displays 'Showing results for Kirkland Signature' with a correction notice, building member confidence and reinforcing search accuracy as a feature. Case study →
